## What This Strategy Does

Long Bitcoin holds a directional long BTC position through perpetual contracts on supported perp DEXes. The vault deposits USDC as margin and opens a long BTC-PERP position. When BTC goes up, the vault's NAV increases. When BTC goes down, NAV decreases.

This is a directional strategy. No hedging, no second leg, no delta-neutral structure. The vault is long BTC.

---

## How It Earns Points

The long BTC position carries <Tooltip tip="The total notional value of outstanding perpetual contract positions. More OI means more exchange points earned.">open interest</Tooltip> on the exchange where it is held. That open interest generates exchange points over time. This is a single-leg strategy (long only, on one exchange).

The credits-per-update amount is reported per EPT and reflects the strategy's OI on the exchange.

---

## The Pitch: Subsidized BTC Exposure

When you deposit into the Long Bitcoin vault, the protocol mints both ST and EPT. You can sell the EPT on the orderbook and recoup part of your deposit. The net effect: your cost of holding a long BTC position is subsidized by the EPT sale.

**Example.** You deposit \$100 into the Long Bitcoin vault. You sell the 100 EPT on the EPT/USDC orderbook for \$0.03 each, recovering \$3. Your effective cost for the BTC exposure is \$97 instead of \$100 — a 3% subsidy on your long position. Over the epoch, BTC rises 5%, so your ST is worth \$105 at redemption.

## What EPT Holders Care About

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Open interest** | OI on the exchange drives credits per update. |
| **Points value at TGE** | The eventual value of exchange points at TGE determines what your EPT is ultimately worth. |
| **EPT price on orderbook** | The market prices EPT based on expected points value. The return depends on what you pay versus what you receive at TGE. |
| **Epoch duration** | Longer epochs and earlier entry mean more credits. |

EPT holders on this strategy are making a bet that the points from the exchange's OI are worth more than what they paid for the EPT.

---

## What ST Holders Care About

ST on the Long Bitcoin strategy is a directional bet on BTC. This is different from funding arb ST, where NAV moves based on funding income.

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **BTC price** | NAV moves directly with BTC. If BTC rises 10%, NAV rises roughly 10%. If BTC falls 10%, NAV falls roughly 10%. |
| **Entry price** | Buying ST at a discount on the ST/USDC orderbook provides a buffer. A 5% discount means BTC can drop 5% and you still break even at redemption. |
| **Epoch duration** | Longer epochs mean more time for BTC to move. This cuts both ways. |
| **Funding costs** | Holding a long perp position may cost funding if funding rates are positive. This drags on NAV over time. |

**Example.** You buy ST at \$0.90 on the orderbook. BTC rises 8% over the epoch. Final NAV is approximately 1.08. You redeem at NAV 1.08. Return: (1.08 - 0.90) / 0.90 = 20%. The 10% discount amplified your BTC exposure.

If BTC falls 8%, final NAV is approximately 0.92. You redeem at NAV 0.92. Return: (0.92 - 0.90) / 0.90 = 2.2%. The discount saved you from a loss.

We are exploring an option for ST holders to redeem for spot BTC at finalization instead of USDC, making this strategy function like a Bitcoin bond: buy discounted exposure now, receive BTC later.

## Risk Profile

This is a directional strategy with 1x leverage. The risk profile is different from delta-neutral strategies.

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| **BTC price decline** | NAV falls when BTC falls. ST holders have full downside exposure. |
| **Funding drag** | If funding rates are persistently positive, the strategy pays funding on its long position, reducing NAV over time even if BTC price is flat. |
| **Exchange risk** | The position is held on a third-party perp DEX. Smart contract risk, downtime, or exchange-level issues affect the strategy. |

Because the strategy runs at 1x leverage (fully collateralized), there is no liquidation risk.

ST holders should treat this as 1x BTC exposure with a known cost basis (their ST purchase price) and a fixed time horizon (the epoch duration).

EPT holders are not exposed to BTC price risk. EPT value depends on points accrual, not strategy PnL.
