## Why Credits Exist

Without credits, points distribution would be trivially gameable. A naive design that distributes points proportional to EPT balance at epoch end lets anyone deposit one second before finalization and claim the same share as a day-one holder.

Credits prevent this by tracking **how much EPT you held** and **for how many ticks** during the epoch. Your share of points is proportional to your cumulative credit balance, not a single end-of-epoch snapshot. A last-second depositor accumulates nearly zero credits and receives a negligible share of points, making the attack economically worthless.

---

## How Tick-Based Credits Work

An off-chain oracle periodically publishes a **tick** to the ArcX settlement contract. Each tick specifies a number: **credits per EPT since the last tick**.

$$
\text{credits}_\text{tick} = \text{EPT balance} \times \text{credits per EPT for that tick}
$$

Every EPT holder's credit balance increases by this amount on each tick. Over an epoch with $n$ ticks, a holder's total credits are:

$$
\text{totalCredits}_i = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \text{balance}_i(k) \times c_k
$$

where $c_k$ is the credits-per-EPT published at tick $k$, and $\text{balance}_i(k)$ is user $i$'s EPT balance at that tick.

### What drives the credit amount per tick?

The credits per tick reflect the strategy's actual activity on the underlying perp DEX during that interval. More open interest means the strategy is generating more exchange points, so the oracle publishes a higher credit amount. When activity drops, credit amounts per tick decrease accordingly.

This means credits are not just time-weighted. They are **time-and-activity-weighted**. Holding EPT during a period of high strategy activity earns more credits per tick than holding during a quiet period.

---

## Fresh Credits on Transfer

When EPT changes hands, whether through a secondary trade on the EPT/USDC orderbook or through minting via deposit, the new holder's credit counter starts from zero at the moment of acquisition.

- **Secondary purchase:** You buy 50 EPT on the orderbook. You start with zero credits. The seller retains all credits they accrued up to the moment of the sale.
- **Minting:** You deposit USDC and receive freshly minted EPT. The new EPT starts at zero credits.
- **No credit inheritance:** Credits are non-transferable. The buyer never receives the seller's accumulated credits.

This is essential for fairness. Without the fresh-start rule, an attacker could buy EPT one tick before finalization and inherit the seller's entire credit history, defeating the purpose of the credit system.

---

## Points Distribution at Finalization

At epoch finalization, the oracle reports the **total points** earned by the strategy during the epoch. Each holder's share is a simple pro-rata calculation:

$$
\text{yourPoints} = \frac{\text{yourCredits}}{\text{totalCredits}} \times \text{totalPoints}
$$

Your credit fraction of the total equals your point fraction of the total.

## Worked Examples

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Two Holders, Different Entry" icon="users">
    **Setup:** 100 ticks at 10 credits/EPT/tick. Total points: 5,000.
    - Alice holds 100 EPT from tick 1 (present for all 100 ticks)
    - Bob buys 100 EPT at tick 61 (present for 40 ticks)

    **Credits:**

    $$
    \text{Alice} = 100 \times 10 \times 100 = 100{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Bob} = 100 \times 10 \times 40 = 40{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Total} = 140{,}000
    $$

    **Points:**

    | Holder | Credits | Share | Points |
    |--------|---------|-------|--------|
    | Alice  | 100,000 | 71.4% | 3,571  |
    | Bob    | 40,000  | 28.6% | 1,429  |

    Alice held the same amount of EPT as Bob, but for 2.5x as many ticks. She gets 2.5x the credits and 2.5x the points.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Variable Tick Amounts" icon="chart-mixed">
    **Setup:** 100 ticks total. Total points: 5,000.
    - First 50 ticks: 20 credits/EPT/tick (high OI period)
    - Last 50 ticks: 5 credits/EPT/tick (low OI period)
    - Alice holds 100 EPT from tick 1 (full epoch)
    - Bob buys 100 EPT at tick 51 (second half only)

    **Credits:**

    $$
    \text{Alice} = (100 \times 20 \times 50) + (100 \times 5 \times 50) = 100{,}000 + 25{,}000 = 125{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Bob} = 100 \times 5 \times 50 = 25{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Total} = 150{,}000
    $$

    **Points:**

    | Holder | Credits | Share | Points |
    |--------|---------|-------|--------|
    | Alice  | 125,000 | 83.3% | 4,167  |
    | Bob    | 25,000  | 16.7% | 833    |

    Compare to constant ticks (previous tab): Alice got 71.4% there vs 83.3% here. With variable ticks, Alice is rewarded more because she held EPT during the high-activity period when the strategy was generating most of its points. Bob only held during the quiet period.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Last-Second Attack" icon="clock">
    **Setup:** 100 ticks at 10 credits/EPT/tick. Total points: 5,000.
    - Alice holds 100 EPT for all 100 ticks
    - Eve buys 100 EPT at tick 100 (the final tick)

    **Credits:**

    $$
    \text{Alice} = 100 \times 10 \times 100 = 100{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Eve} = 100 \times 10 \times 1 = 1{,}000
    $$

    $$
    \text{Total} = 101{,}000
    $$

    **Points:**

    | Holder | Credits | Share | Points |
    |--------|---------|-------|--------|
    | Alice  | 100,000 | 99.0% | 4,950  |
    | Eve    | 1,000   | 1.0%  | 50     |

    Eve deposited the same dollar amount as Alice but accumulated credits for only 1 out of 100 ticks. She receives 1% of points. The attack is economically neutralized.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
