## The Problem

Points farming on perp DEXes is capital-inefficient. You deposit capital, lock it in a strategy, earn points alongside PnL, and hope for an airdrop. You can't sell the points pre-TGE, you can't separate the yield from the points, and you can't get leveraged exposure to either one independently.

## The Solution

ArcX splits the output of a perp trading strategy into two tokens, **ST** (claim on USDC returns) and **EPT** (claim on exchange points), and runs a separate orderbook for each. Points farmers buy EPT. Yield seekers buy discounted ST. Each user gets exactly the exposure they want, at a price set by the market.

**ST** tracks the per-share USDC value of the underlying strategy. **EPT** accrues credits while you hold it. Your share of points at finalization equals your credits divided by total credits. **PointsToken** is the final output: one token per point, redeemable at TGE.

## How It Works

### The Minting Equation

$$1 \text{ USDC} = \frac{1}{R} \text{ ST} + 1 \text{ EPT}$$

where $R$ is the current <Tooltip tip="Net Asset Value. The per-share USDC value of the strategy vault.">NAV</Tooltip> per share. Depositing 1 USDC into a strategy mints both tokens together.

### Two Orderbooks, One Matching Engine

ArcX runs two separate orderbooks: **EPT/USDC** and **ST/USDC**. Orders are matched off-chain and settled on-chain (Starknet).

**Cross-matching for mints.** When a buy order on the EPT book and a buy order on the ST book have prices that sum to at least the minting cost, the engine cross-matches them. The protocol takes the combined USDC, deposits it into the vault, and delivers EPT to one buyer and ST to the other. Neither buyer had to deposit into the strategy directly.

**Secondary trades.** Within each orderbook, buyers and sellers trade existing tokens. No minting or burning occurs.

### EPT Pricing

The fair value of EPT is derived from the ST price:

$$\text{EPT}_{\text{fair}} = 1 - \frac{X}{R}$$

where $X$ is the ST market price and $R$ is the current NAV. As the epoch progresses, EPT price tends to decay. EPT is an expiring claim on remaining points, and the market prices it accordingly.

### Credits and Points

EPT accrues **credits** over time. An oracle periodically reports accumulated credits per EPT, derived from the strategy's open interest on the exchange. Credits start fresh on acquisition. A buyer starts from zero regardless of the token's history.

At finalization:

$$\text{your points} = \frac{\text{your credits}}{\text{total credits}} \times \text{total points}$$

## Direct Minting

Instead of buying on the orderbook, you can deposit USDC directly into a strategy. You receive both $\frac{1}{R}$ ST and 1 EPT per USDC deposited, minus a small fee (varies by strategy). This is useful if you want exposure to both tokens, but most users will prefer buying the specific token they want on the orderbook.

## Epochs

Each strategy runs in fixed epochs (typically 8-12 weeks) that progress through three stages:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Active">
    <Badge color="green">ACTIVE</Badge>

    Orderbooks are open. Credits accrue. The strategy is running.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Maturity">
    <Badge color="orange">MATURITY</Badge>

    Strategy unwinds. No new deposits. Orderbooks close.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finalized">
    <Badge color="red">FINALIZED</Badge>

    Final NAV and points are published. ST redeems for USDC. EPT claims <Tooltip tip="1:1 on-chain representation of exchange points. Persists across epochs. Burn for airdrop tokens after TGE.">PointsTokens</Tooltip>.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Epochs are independent. There is no automatic rollover. See [Epoch Lifecycle](/learn/epoch-lifecycle) for details.

## Key Formulas

| Formula | Definition |
|---|---|
| $\text{NAV} = \frac{\text{vault value}}{\text{total shares}}$ | Per-share value of the strategy |
| $\text{shares} = \frac{\text{deposit amount}}{\text{NAV}}$ | ST shares received on deposit |
| $\text{EPT}_{\text{fair}} = 1 - \frac{X}{R}$ | Fair value of EPT given ST price $X$ and NAV $R$ |
| $\text{your points} = \frac{\text{your credits}}{\text{total credits}} \times \text{total points}$ | Points allocation at finalization |
