## What Is EPT

EPT is your claim on exchange points earned by a strategy during the epoch. Each epoch creates a new EPT per exchange per strategy. When you deposit USDC, you receive both ST and EPT. When the epoch finalizes, your EPT entitles you to a share of the total points earned, proportional to the credits you accrued. You then claim your points as <Tooltip tip="1:1 on-chain representation of exchange points. Persists across epochs. Burn for airdrop tokens after TGE." href="/learn/points-token">PointsTokens</Tooltip>.

EPT trades on the **EPT/USDC orderbook**. You can buy more EPT to increase your points exposure, or sell EPT if you only want the USDC yield from ST.

If a strategy earns points on multiple exchanges, you receive a separate EPT for each.

---

## The Credit System

Credits determine your share of points. The system is tick-based: an off-chain oracle periodically pushes a **credit tick** reporting credits per EPT since the last tick, derived from the strategy's open interest and activity on the exchange.

While you hold EPT, your credits accumulate based on these oracle updates. The key rules:

- **Credits are personal.** They belong to the holder, not the token.
- **Fresh start on acquisition.** Whether you mint new EPT or buy it on the orderbook, your credit counter for those tokens starts from zero. The seller's accumulated credits stay with the seller.
- **No transfer of credits.** When EPT changes hands, the seller keeps all credits earned up to that point. The buyer begins accruing from their moment of acquisition.

This means earlier holders accumulate more credits per EPT than later holders, all else being equal.

---

## How Credits Determine Your Points

At finalization, the total points earned by the strategy are reported. Your share is:

$$\text{your\_points} = \frac{\text{your\_credits}}{\text{total\_credits}} \times \text{total\_points}$$

Everyone's credits are summed into the denominator. Your fraction of that sum determines your fraction of the points.

## Worked Example

Alice and Bob each hold 100 EPT in an epoch with 100 ticks at a constant 10 credits/EPT/tick. Alice holds from tick 1. Bob buys on the orderbook at tick 61 (his credits start from zero at purchase). Total points for the epoch: 5,000.

| | Holding period | Credits accrued | Share of total | Points (of 5,000) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alice | 100 ticks (full epoch) | 100,000 | 71.4% | 3,571 |
| Bob | 40 ticks (tick 61 onward) | 40,000 | 28.6% | 1,429 |

Alice earns more points because she held for 2.5x as many ticks. The credit system weights **amount held times ticks held**. There is no bonus or multiplier for early entry. Accrual is linear.

---

## Why Tick Amounts Vary

The oracle does not report a fixed credits-per-EPT value. It adjusts each tick based on real strategy activity:

| Strategy type | Tick amount reflects |
|---|---|
| Funding arbitrage | Current open interest |
| Market making | Trading volume throughput |

If a strategy is idle for 3 weeks then active for 5 weeks, tick amounts are low during the idle period and high during the active period. This concentrates credit accrual during the windows when points are actually being generated on the exchange.

For the full mathematical treatment, see [Credit Mathematics](/deep-dives/credit-mathematics).

## EPT Pricing

EPT has a fair value determined by the minting relationship between ST and EPT:

$$\text{EPT}_{\text{fair}} = 1 - \frac{X}{R}$$

Where:
- **X** = ST market price on the ST/USDC orderbook
- **R** = <Tooltip tip="Net Asset Value. The per-share USDC value of the strategy vault.">NAV</Tooltip> (net asset value per share)

**Intuition:** A \$1 deposit creates both ST and EPT. If ST trades at \$0.92 and NAV is \$1.00, the implied EPT value from minting parity is \$0.08. On the orderbook, actual EPT prices are set by supply and demand between points farmers and sellers.

### The Interest Rate Frame

Rather than asking "is \$0.10 too much for an EPT?", think about the **implied return rate**. EPT decays in value over the epoch as time remaining decreases (fewer credits left to earn). The EPT price at any moment reflects the market's view of the remaining points value. Early in the epoch, the same EPT costs more because there are more credits left to accrue.

Full pricing analysis and decay mechanics are covered in [EPT Pricing](/deep-dives/ept-pricing).

## Claiming Points

After the epoch finalizes, claim your share as [PointsTokens](/learn/points-token). Your allocation is based on your [credit share](#how-credits-determine-your-points). A small fee (varies by strategy) is deducted, and PointsTokens are minted to your wallet.

Each epoch produces a separate EPT. You claim from each epoch independently. Credits do not carry over between epochs.
