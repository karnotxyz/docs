## What Is EPT

EPT is your claim on exchange points earned by a strategy during the epoch. Each epoch creates a new EPT per exchange per strategy. When you deposit USDC, you receive both ST and EPT. When the epoch finalizes, your EPT entitles you to a share of the total points earned, proportional to the credits you accrued.

EPT trades on the **EPT/USDC orderbook**. You can buy more EPT to increase your points exposure, or sell EPT if you only want the USDC yield from ST.

If a strategy earns points on multiple exchanges, you receive a separate EPT for each.

---

## The Credit System

Credits determine your share of points. The system is tick-based: an off-chain oracle periodically reports the current **credits per EPT**, derived from the strategy's open interest and activity on the exchange.

While you hold EPT, your credits accumulate based on these oracle updates. The key rules:

- **Credits are personal.** They belong to the holder, not the token.
- **Fresh start on acquisition.** Whether you mint new EPT or buy it on the orderbook, your credit counter for those tokens starts from zero. The seller's accumulated credits stay with the seller.
- **No transfer of credits.** When EPT changes hands, the seller keeps all credits earned up to that point. The buyer begins accruing from their moment of acquisition.

This means earlier holders accumulate more credits per EPT than later holders, all else being equal.

---

## How Credits Determine Your Points

At finalization, the total points earned by the strategy are reported. Your share is:

$$\text{your\_points} = \frac{\text{your\_credits}}{\text{total\_credits}} \times \text{total\_points}$$

Everyone's credits are summed into the denominator. Your fraction of that sum determines your fraction of the points.

---

## Worked Example

Alice and Bob each hold 100 EPT in an 8-week epoch. Alice holds from Day 0. Bob buys on the orderbook at Week 3 (his credits start from zero at purchase).

Assume the oracle reports a constant credit rate for simplicity.

| | Holding period | Credits accrued | Share of total | Points (of 1,000) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alice | 8 weeks (full epoch) | 604,800 | 63.6% | 636 |
| Bob | 5 weeks (Week 3 onward) | 345,600 | 36.4% | 364 |

Alice earns more points because she held longer and accrued credits for 3 additional weeks. The credit system weights **amount held times duration held**. There is no bonus or multiplier for early entry. Accrual is linear.

---

## Why Variable Credit Rates

The oracle does not report a fixed rate. It adjusts based on real strategy activity:

| Strategy type | Credit rate reflects |
|---|---|
| Funding arbitrage | Current open interest |
| Market making | Trading volume throughput |

If a strategy is idle for 3 weeks then active for 5 weeks, the credit rate is low during the idle period and high during the active period. This concentrates credit accrual during the windows when points are actually being generated on the exchange.

For the full mathematical treatment, see [Credit Mathematics](/deep-dives/credit-mathematics).

## EPT Pricing

EPT has a fair value determined by the minting relationship between ST and EPT:

$$\text{EPT}_{\text{fair}} = 1 - \frac{X}{R}$$

Where:
- **X** = ST market price on the ST/USDC orderbook
- **R** = NAV (net asset value per share)

**Intuition:** A $1 deposit creates both ST and EPT. If ST trades at $0.90 and NAV is $1.00, then the implied EPT cost is $0.10 per token. The deeper the ST discount, the higher the implied EPT cost, because the points farmer is giving up more USDC value on the ST side.

### The Interest Rate Frame

Rather than asking "is $0.10 too much for an EPT?", think about the **implied return rate**. EPT decays in value over the epoch as time remaining decreases (fewer credits left to earn). The EPT price at any moment reflects the market's view of the remaining points value. Early in the epoch, the same EPT costs more because there are more credits left to accrue.

Full pricing analysis and decay mechanics are covered in [EPT Pricing](/deep-dives/ept-pricing).

## Cross-Matching: How Minting Works

The ST/USDC and EPT/USDC orderbooks are separate, but they share a minting mechanism. When a new deposit occurs, it creates both ST and EPT. The protocol's matching engine can cross-match across the two books:

- A buyer on the EPT/USDC book and a buyer on the ST/USDC book can together trigger a mint, each receiving the token they want
- Secondary trades (EPT seller to EPT buyer, or ST seller to ST buyer) happen within each respective book

This means liquidity on one book affects pricing on the other through the minting parity relationship.

## Claiming Points

After the epoch finalizes, the total points are reported and you can claim your share as [PointsTokens](/learn/points-token).

Your claim is based on your credit share:

$$\text{your\_PointsTokens} = \frac{\text{your\_credits}}{\text{total\_credits}} \times \text{total\_points}$$

A small fee (varies by strategy) is deducted, and the net PointsTokens are minted to your wallet.

Each epoch's EPT is a separate contract. You claim from each epoch independently. Credits do not carry over between epochs.
